How does card scanning work?

Our app uses advanced OCR (Optical Character Recognition) technology powered by AI to identify Pokemon cards from photos. Simply point your camera at a card, and the app will automatically detect and identify it using the card's text, images, and layout.

Why isn't my card being recognized?

Card recognition issues can occur due to:

  • Poor lighting conditions
  • Blurry or tilted photos
  • Damaged or heavily played cards
  • Non-English cards (currently we support English cards primarily)
  • Very rare or custom cards not in our database

Tips: Ensure good lighting, hold the camera steady, and try different angles.

Is my collection data safe?

Yes! Your collection data is:

  • Stored locally on your device by default
  • Encrypted during cloud sync (premium feature)
  • Never shared with third parties
  • Backed up securely on our servers (if cloud sync enabled)
  • Exportable at any time in standard formats

Yes! Premium users can import collections from:

  • CSV files with card names and quantities
  • Other popular collection apps (contact support for specific formats)
  • Manual entry with search and autocomplete
  • Bulk photo scanning mode

App Performance Issues

If the app is running slowly or crashing:

  1. Close and restart the app
  2. Restart your device
  3. Ensure you have the latest app version
  4. Free up device storage space (at least 1GB recommended)
  5. Check your internet connection

Camera/Scanning Problems

If camera scanning isn't working:

  1. Check app permissions in device settings
  2. Clean your camera lens
  3. Ensure adequate lighting
  4. Try different angles and distances
  5. Restart the app

Sync Issues

If your data isn't syncing between devices:

  1. Verify you're signed in to the same account
  2. Check your internet connection
  3. Force close and reopen the app
  4. Manually trigger sync in settings
  5. Contact support if issues persist
